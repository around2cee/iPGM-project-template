# 2.5 Communications Plan
> **PGM Role:** Creator & Collaborator | **Phase:** Planning

Framework for who needs what information, when, via what channel, and by whom.
This is not the messages themselves — it is the plan for how communication happens.

---

## Communications Matrix

| Audience | Information Type | Frequency | Format / Channel | Owner | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Core Team | Task updates, blockers | Weekly | Team meeting + notes in meeting-notes/ | PGM | |
| Program Sponsor | Status, risks, decisions needed | Bi-weekly | Status report + 1:1 | PGM | |
| Steering Committee | Program health, approvals | Monthly | SteerCo pack | PGM | |
| Leadership / Executives | Headline status | Monthly | Executive update | PGM | |
| All Stakeholders | Major milestones, go-live | As needed | Email / Slack | PGM | |
| Engineering Teams | Technical decisions, scope | As needed | Confluence / Slack | Tech Lead | |

---

## Escalation Path
| Situation | First Contact | Escalation to | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|
| Missed deliverable | Workstream Lead | Program Sponsor | Same day |
| Budget overrun | Program Manager | iPGM Lead + Sponsor | Same day |
| Critical risk materialized | Program Manager | Steering Committee | Within 24 hrs |

---

## Meeting Cadence
| Meeting | Attendees | Frequency | Notes Location |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core team sync | Core team | Weekly | meeting-notes/ |
| Sponsor check-in | PGM + Sponsor | Bi-weekly | meeting-notes/ |
| Steering Committee | Execs + Sponsor + PGM | Monthly | steering-committee-packs/ |

---

_For large programs, break this into its own document. For most programs, this table is sufficient._
