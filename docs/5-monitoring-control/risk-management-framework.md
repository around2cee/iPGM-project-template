# 5.3 Risk Management Framework
> **PGM Role:** Creator & Owner | **Phase:** Planning → Closure (ongoing)

Defines how risks are identified, assessed, tracked, escalated, and resolved
across the life of this program.

Answers: "How do we systematically prevent bad surprises, and how do we respond when risk materializes?"

---

## Risk Identification
Risks are identified by asking:
1. Where could this go wrong?
2. How bad would it be, and how likely?
3. What are we doing about it?
4. Is the risk changing?
5. Who needs to know, and who decides?

Sources: team retrospectives, dependency reviews, assumption validation, stakeholder feedback.

## Risk Assessment Matrix

| Likelihood | Low Impact | Medium Impact | High Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| **High** | Yellow | Orange | Red |
| **Medium** | Green | Yellow | Orange |
| **Low** | Green | Green | Yellow |

## Risk Response Types
| Response | When to Use |
|---|---|
| Avoid | Change the plan to eliminate the risk entirely |
| Mitigate | Reduce likelihood or impact |
| Transfer | Shift risk to a third party (insurance, contract) |
| Accept | Acknowledge the risk, monitor, no active action |

## Escalation Thresholds
| Risk Level | Action |
|---|---|
| Red | Immediate escalation to Program Sponsor + SteerCo |
| Orange | Escalate to Program Sponsor within 48 hours |
| Yellow | Manage within core team, review weekly |
| Green | Monitor only |
